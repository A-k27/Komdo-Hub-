THE SUMATRAN TIGER

Illegal Wildlife Trade


The primary reason for the deliberate killing of most Sumatran tigers is driven by monetary gains. TRAFFIC, a global network monitoring wildlife trade, has reported that nearly 80% of estimated Sumatran tiger deaths, totaling at least 40 animals per year, result from poaching for commercial motives.


Despite escalated conservation and protection endeavors in specific regions of Sumatra and some success in diminishing the market for tiger parts, there is limited evidence to suggest a significant decline in tiger poaching on the island since the early 1990s.


Human-Wildlife Conflict

Similar to other regions where tigers reside, Sumatra faces a significant issue with human-tiger conflicts. This situation has resulted in casualties and injuries among people, and tigers sometimes prey on livestock. In response, villagers might take retaliatory measures that lead to the killing of tigers.
