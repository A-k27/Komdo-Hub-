THE JAVAN RHINOCEROS

Reduced Genetic Diversity


The limited number of Javan rhinos raises alarm. Reduced genetic variation and inbreeding might pose challenges for the species' prolonged existence.


Disasters


The Ujung Kulon National Park along the coast faces significant susceptibility to tsunamis, with a potential catastrophic impact if the nearby Anak Krakatau volcano were to experience a major eruption, potentially eradicating much of the life within the protected area. Additionally, the park is at risk due to rising sea levels caused by climate change.


Disease


Over the past few years, it is believed that four rhinos, among them a young adult female, perished due to an illness likely transmitted initially to wild cattle within the park and then passed on to the rhinos.


Habitat Degradation


Residents residing near the park are encroaching upon and deteriorating essential rhino habitats. Substantial economic growth in the vicinity of the park, including (eco)tourism, poses a risk of additional intrusion into the park area.
